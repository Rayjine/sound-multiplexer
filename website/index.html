Tick the outputs you want and sound follows. The app builds the audio plumbing for you — and removes it when you close it.

Two-way sync

Native and small

How it works

On Linux, the app drives the audio server you already have. Select two or more devices and it creates a combined PulseAudio/PipeWire sink and makes it the default — your volume keys control it like any other output. Drop back to one device and the plumbing disappears again; quitting cleans everything up. On Windows, a WASAPI engine mirrors the default output to every other selected device.
